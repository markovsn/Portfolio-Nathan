Australian Koala Foundation

Website redesign

Challenge

The Australian Koala Foundation's (AKF) website lacks a brand personality that promotes trust and transparency, which users seek when visiting non-for-profit websites and donating.

Solution

Redesign the homepage and donation flow by promoting donation as the primary call to action, streamlining the donation process, and adding elements that build trust and transparency with the user.

My role

Conducted user tests, prepared the competitor analysis, journey map, user flow and feature priortisaiton matrix, and led the design of the navigation bar and homepage.

Timing

Three weeks

Research

UX Research

I worked with my team to conduct five guerilla tests and redlining of the existing website, a Goolge survey, and a competitor analysis.

Research findings

Our research found that:

  • The current website is disorderly, difficult to navigate, and does not inspire users to contribute,
  • Over 90 per cent of users surveyed would rather provide a financial contribution than non-financial
  • Users seek trust, transparency and purpose when visitng non-for-profit websites,
  • Donation pages of other non-for-profits are simple and reduce user choice overload.
    • How might we improve the donation process so users feel encouraged to donate and assured that their contribution will be used to directly support the cause?

    Information architecture

    Persona

    We developed a persona, Sarah Lisgow, to better understand the needs and goals of our user. Sarah is willing to make a small donation, however, she seeks trust and assurance that her money will be spent appropriately on the cause.

    Feature prioritisation

    I developed a feature prioritisation matrix to prioritise our work based on the importance to the organisation and the time needed to implement.

    Since we had limited time, our team agreed to focus on optimising the donation process and redesign high-impact pages such as the homepage and about us page.

    Journey Map

    I created a journey map to highlight the key stages of our users journey and oppotunities to improve the user experience along the way. A key focus is to build trust and transparency to encourage the users to donate.

    For example, showing users how donation money has been spent to date at the point of action could increase the doantion conversion rate.

    User Flow

    I developed a user flow to highlight the core paths a user would take to make a contribution to AKF. The five core options to contribute are donate, plant a tree, adopt a koala, volunteer and join the koala army. Our focus was to improve the donate process.

    Building

    Sketching and wireframing

    I led the redesign of the homepage. I initially created a low-fidelity wireframe that I shared with my team and friends for early feedback. I then proceeded with a mid-fidelity wireframe to better visualise the design and create a clickable prototype for testing.

    Information architecture

    While not an immediate focus, I felt the navigation bar could be improved by consolidating the number of primary links. We conducted two rounds of card sorting to identify opportunities to achive a simple nav bar.

    A new site map was developed to capture four broad categories, while maintaining the search icon and donate button as standalone elements for greater visibility.

    Responsive design

    A 12 column grid was applied to all screens. Breakpoints were applied at ‘max-width: 950px’ for tablet and ‘max-width: 550px’ for mobile.

    The navigation bar was hidden behind a burger menu from tablet mode, however, the donate button remained viewable to promote the CTA.

    Style Guide

    I developed a style guide to showcase the new AKF branding. The branding colours to reflect the Australian symbolism of the koala.

    Final design

    Homepage

    The homepage was redesigned using cards and containers to provide structure and reduce visual noise. Sections were reordered based on user needs. A collapsable section for emergency contacts appears first, followed by news and updates from the Department.

    Donation pages

    A horizontal navigation bar was maintained to save space on the homepage and provide more room for the dropdowns. Nav links were condensed into four broad categories to reduce choice overload.
