CURIADO

Mobile application

Challenge

Users struggle to find the right information when starting a creative hobby, which makes it harder for them to get started and avoid overspending.

Solution

Curiado helps users find and develop their creative passions by providing hobby-specific guides and advice.

My role

Leading user research and interviews, feature prioritisation and value proposition, user flow, wireframing the onboarding and forum screens, co-creating the UI design and branding, conducting user testing.

Timing

Three weeks

Research

User research

Research suggests 45 per cent of Australians participate in a creative hobby, of these, half are between the ages of 15 and 34.

My team and I interviewed seven people in this demographic as potential users of our product, as we wanted to understand common frustrations people face when starting and maintaining a creative hobby.

We also received 71 responses to our Instagram survey to obtain additional data on hobbyists.

User Insights

We found that creative hobbyists:

  • overspend on items they don’t need or use,
  • struggle to find reliable and relevant information when starting a creative hobby,
  • quickly lose motivation when learning a creative hobby becomes challenging, and
  • like to share their creations on social media.

    • How might we provide users with reliable information so they are informed and confident to participate in a creative hobby?

    Ideation

    Persona

    We developed a persona to better define our target user based on our research findings. Mapping out our personas goals and pain-points allowed us to brainstorm and prioritise solutions.

    Feature prioritisation

    I facilitated a brainstorming session with my team. Potential features were ranked on a matrix agaist necessity and time to implement. We decided to focus our limited time on:

  • onboarding screens,
  • a feature for users to view guides and tutorials for various hobbies, and
  • a forum for hobbysitst to interact and share their creations with other hobbyists.

    • Competitor analysis

    An analysis of competitors’ features revealed a gap in competitors that offer both guides and forums.

    Competitors that offer guides generally charge an access fee or, if free, include affiliated links that diminish credibility.

    While there are a number of established competitors in the forum space, they are either broad-ranging (e.g. Reddit) or hobby-specific (e.g. GuitarForums.com).

    User flow

    I created a user flow to showcase the core tasks users could undertake. Forums and Guides are free to access to increase user participation. Other key tasks, such as favouriting, require user registration and/or sign in to access.

    Building

    Sketching and wireframing

    I led the design of the forums. I envisioned users could vertically scroll a menu of small container previews of forum posts. Users can favourite posts they enjoy, which also helps the post to better rank against others.

    Testing

    I conducted two rounds of user tests address any problems users faced when navigating the forums and completing core tasks.

    The floating button was resized and would vanish on upward scroll to be less distracting. Forum post previews were redesigned to be easier for users to intepret and complete their tasks.

    Style Guide

    I developed a style guide to showcase the key branding elements for the app design. The branding takes a 'hand drawn' effect with the abundant use of white and black outlines. Bright colours are used sparringly to signify a fun and energy.

    Final design

    Onboarding

    Users can finalise their plans by creating an itinerary. Users can add items manually or upload their booking confirmations, which automatically extracts key information. Itineraries can be edited collaboratively, printed and shared.

    Guides

    Users can create a trip and invite their fellow travellers to collaboratively plan their trip. Users can add, archive and delete items and vote for their favourite items to facilitate consensus

    Forums

    Users can search for places and things to do. Carousels are used to allow users to quickly navigate across (by scrolling down) and within (by scrolling left and right) cateogories. Items can be easily bookmarked by swiping right
